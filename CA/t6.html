The Himachal Pradesh Cricket Association (HPCA) Stadium in Dharamshala is famous for its picturesque backdrop of the Dhauladhar mountain range. This region offers numerous attractions showcasing the natural beauty, spirituality, and cultural richness of the Himalayas. Here are some top tourist spots near HPCA Stadium:



1. McLeod Ganj

  • Distance from Stadium: Approximately 5 km

  • Highlights: Known as “Little Lhasa,” McLeod Ganj is the home of the Dalai Lama and the Tibetan government-in-exile. The area is filled with Tibetan monasteries, cafes, handicraft markets, and vibrant Tibetan culture. Key attractions include the Tsuglagkhang Complex (the Dalai Lama’s temple), Bhagsu Waterfall, and Bhagsunath Temple.

  • Ideal for: Cultural enthusiasts, spiritual seekers, and those interested in Tibetan heritage.

2. Namgyal Monastery

  • Distance from Stadium: About 5 km

  • Highlights: Located within the Tsuglagkhang Complex, Namgyal Monastery is one of the most important monasteries in Tibetan Buddhism. It’s the personal monastery of the Dalai Lama and provides a tranquil space for meditation, learning, and observing Tibetan religious ceremonies.

  • Ideal for: Those interested in Buddhism, meditation, and spiritual practices.

3. Dal Lake

  • Distance from Stadium: Around 6 km

  • Highlights: Surrounded by deodar trees and situated amidst the Himalayas, Dal Lake is a serene, green lake ideal for a peaceful outing. A Shiva temple is located nearby, adding a cultural touch to this scenic spot. It’s a popular location for picnics and short treks.

  • Ideal for: Nature lovers, families, and photographers.

4. Kangra Fort

  • Distance from Stadium: Approximately 20 km

  • Highlights: The Kangra Fort is one of India’s oldest forts, built by the royal family of Kangra. It offers a fascinating glimpse into the region’s history and is strategically perched with breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys and mountains. The fort complex includes temples, a museum, and several ancient structures.

  • Ideal for: History buffs, architecture enthusiasts, and photographers.

5. Triund Hill

  • Distance from Stadium: About 10 km (starting point for the trek)

  • Highlights: Triund is a popular trekking destination known for its panoramic views of the Dhauladhar mountain range and the Kangra Valley. The trek begins from McLeod Ganj or Dharamkot, taking around 4-5 hours to reach the summit. The trek offers beautiful scenic views and is suitable for beginner to moderate-level trekkers.

  • Ideal for: Adventure enthusiasts, trekkers, and nature lovers.
