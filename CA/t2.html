The M. A. Chidambaram Stadium in Chennai, Tamil Nadu, also known as Chepauk Stadium, is one of India’s most iconic cricket venues. Located in the heart of Chennai, it offers easy access to numerous nearby attractions that showcase the city's rich history, spirituality, culture, and scenic beauty. Here are some must-visit places near the stadium:



1.Marina Beach

  • Distance from Stadium: 1.5 km

  • Highlights: Stretching over 13 km, Marina Beach is one of the longest urban beaches in the world. It’s a popular spot for both locals and tourists to enjoy the sunrise, take walks along the sandy shores, and savor street food like sundal and murukku. Statues, memorials, and the iconic lighthouse add to its charm. Evenings here are vibrant with food vendors and cultural performances.

2.Fort St. George

  • Distance from Stadium: 3 km

  • Highlights: Built in 1644, Fort St. George is a historically significant site that marks the beginning of British influence in India. The fort houses the Fort Museum, showcasing artifacts from the British colonial period, including cannons, paintings, and documents. The St. Mary’s Church, one of India’s oldest Anglican churches, is also within the fort premises.

3.Kapaleeshwarar Temple

  • Distance from Stadium: 6 km

  • Highlights: A stunning example of Dravidian architecture, the Kapaleeshwarar Temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva. Known for its towering gopurams (ornate entrance towers), intricately carved pillars, and vibrant sculptures, it’s a must-visit for those interested in Tamil spirituality and culture. The temple is especially lively during festivals, with local devotees and visitors flocking to admire its splendor.

4.San Thome Basilica

  • Distance from Stadium: 4 km

  • Highlights: This beautiful, white neo-Gothic church is believed to house the tomb of Saint Thomas the Apostle, one of Jesus Christ's twelve apostles. Built in the 16th century by Portuguese explorers and later rebuilt by the British, it’s a popular pilgrimage site for Christians. Inside, visitors can admire its stained glass windows, relics, and a peaceful crypt.

5. Government Museum (Egmore)

  • Distance from Stadium: 5 km

  • Highlights: One of India’s oldest museums, the Government Museum in Egmore has extensive collections of archaeology, zoology, botany, and anthropology. Highlights include the Bronze Gallery, featuring iconic bronze statues from the Chola dynasty, and exhibits on ancient South Indian art and culture. The museum complex also houses the National Art Gallery, making it ideal for art and history enthusiasts.
