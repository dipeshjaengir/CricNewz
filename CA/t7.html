The Maharashtra Cricket Association (MCA) Stadium in Pune is surrounded by a mix of historic sites, spiritual retreats, and beautiful natural spots that highlight the region’s rich heritage and scenic beauty. Here are five top tourist attractions near the MCA Stadium:



1. Aga Khan Palace

  • Distance from Stadium: Approximately 20 km

  • Highlights: Built in 1892, Aga Khan Palace is a historical landmark associated with India's freedom struggle. It served as a prison for Mahatma Gandhi and other leaders during the Quit India Movement. The palace showcases artifacts and photos of Mahatma Gandhi and is set amidst beautiful gardens.

  • Ideal for: History enthusiasts and those interested in India’s independence movement.

2. Shaniwar Wada

  • Distance from Stadium: About 24 km

  • Highlights: This 18th-century fortification was built by the Peshwas of the Maratha Empire and is known for its intricate architectural design, majestic gates, and historical significance. Though most of it was destroyed by fire, the remaining structures and the beautiful garden are a testament to Maratha architecture.

  • Ideal for: History buffs, architecture enthusiasts, and photographers.

3. Sinhagad Fort

  • Distance from Stadium: Around 40 km

  • Highlights: Perched atop a hill, Sinhagad Fort offers breathtaking views of the surrounding valley and is a popular destination for trekkers and nature lovers. The fort has a storied history, especially related to Shivaji Maharaj’s battles. Visitors enjoy scenic trekking routes and traditional Maharashtrian snacks at the top.

  • Ideal for: Adventure seekers, history lovers, and trekkers.

4. Osho Ashram

  • Distance from Stadium: Approximately 21 km

  • Highlights: Located in the Koregaon Park area, the Osho Ashram is a tranquil meditation retreat offering various courses in spirituality, meditation, and wellness. With beautiful gardens, a Zen-inspired ambiance, and serene spaces for meditation, it attracts spiritual seekers from around the world.

  • Ideal for: Spiritual seekers, meditation enthusiasts, and those looking for relaxation.

5. Pataleshwar Cave Temple

  • Distance from Stadium: About 25 km

  • Highlights: This 8th-century rock-cut cave temple dedicated to Lord Shiva is an archaeological wonder. The temple features a monolithic structure with intricate carvings, a Nandi bull statue, and peaceful surroundings. It’s a perfect spot for experiencing Pune’s ancient architectural heritage.

  • Ideal for: History and architecture enthusiasts, and those interested in ancient Indian temples.
