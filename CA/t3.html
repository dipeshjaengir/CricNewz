The Wankhede Stadium in Mumbai is a legendary cricket venue with a vibrant setting along the city's scenic coast. Located in South Mumbai, it’s surrounded by iconic attractions, historic sites, and cultural hotspots that showcase the best of Mumbai’s spirit and heritage. Here are some top tourist places near Wankhede Stadium that are worth exploring:



1. Marine Drive

  • Distance from Stadium: About 500 meters

  • Highlights: Known as the "Queen's Necklace" due to its crescent shape and twinkling lights at night, Marine Drive is a 3.6-km-long boulevard along the Arabian Sea. It's perfect for a peaceful walk, a run, or simply sitting by the sea and watching the sunset. The stretch is lively with locals, tourists, and food vendors, especially during evenings.

  • Ideal for: A relaxing seaside experience and panoramic views of the Mumbai skyline.

2. Gateway of India

  • Distance from Stadium: Approximately 2 km

  • Highlights: Built in 1924, the iconic Gateway of India stands as a symbol of Mumbai and a key historical landmark. It faces the Arabian Sea and offers stunning views, especially at dawn and dusk. From here, visitors can catch ferry rides to Elephanta Island or simply enjoy the surrounding area, which includes the famous Taj Mahal Palace Hotel.

  • deal for: History buffs, photographers, and those looking for a memorable view of the Mumbai harbor.

3. Colaba Causeway

  • Distance from Stadium: About 2 km

  • Highlights: This bustling shopping street is famous for its lively markets, colonial architecture, and a variety of cafes and restaurants. It's a fantastic place to shop for souvenirs, jewelry, clothing, and handicrafts. Popular eateries like Leopold Cafe and Cafe Mondegar add to the experience, making it a hotspot for both shopping and dining.

  • Ideal for: Shopaholics, street shoppers, and those wanting a taste of Mumbai’s vibrant street culture.

4. Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Vastu Sangrahalaya

  • Distance from Stadium: 1.5 km

  • Highlights: This museum, housed in a beautiful Indo-Saracenic building, offers a vast collection of Indian and foreign artifacts, including art, sculpture, textiles, and ancient artifacts. It’s one of Mumbai’s premier museums and provides an insightful look into India’s rich history, culture, and art.

  • Ideal for: Art enthusiasts, history lovers, and anyone interested in India’s cultural heritage.

5. Flora Fountain and Fort Area

  • Distance from Stadium: Approximately 1 km

  • Highlights: Flora Fountain, a beautifully sculpted 19th-century monument, is located in the heart of South Mumbai's Fort business district. Surrounded by colonial buildings, it’s a gateway to the city’s British-era architecture and landmarks. Walking around the Fort area, visitors can see historic sites like the Bombay High Court, Rajabai Clock Tower, and the Asiatic Society Library.

  • Ideal for:Architecture aficionados and those interested in exploring Mumbai's colonial history.
